THE ABSOLUTE LIMIT

A project page for the world-ending relief-station narrative, presented as a special project under the official site structure.

PressureRelief StationEmpathy
Overview

Urban collapse under human pressure.

Kevin, a 19-year-old student, enters a relief station environment where scarcity, mistakes, crowd pressure and exhaustion escalate into an emotional breaking point. The project’s strength lies in grounded stress rather than spectacle.

Structure

A concise project structure page keeps the presentation official and readable.

01

Collapse

The city loses stability and the relief station becomes one of the few functioning service points.

02

Overload

Requests intensify. Kevin rotates through supplies, water, meals and crowd handling without rest.

03

Breaking Point

Noise, alarms, heat, fear and panic converge, nearly collapsing his emotional control.

04

Reframing

Empathy and communication restore function. Kevin returns with more purpose and less paralysis.

Stress Response

The project explores overload as a practical and emotional survival condition.

Community Function

The station only survives through cooperation, not individual heroics.

Human Value

Kindness remains meaningful even when institutions and infrastructure are failing.
